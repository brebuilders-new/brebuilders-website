## REPAIRS PAGE CONTENT (verbatim)

**H1:** Structural Repairs in Reno

**H2:** How to Know When It's Serious

**Intro:**
> Not every crack, shift, or uneven surface means your home has a structural problem. In Reno and Lake Tahoe homes, some issues are cosmetic, while others require professional inspection.

**Trust badges:** ✔ Prompt Response  ✔ Skilled Craftsmanship  ✔ Long-Term Solutions

**Body:**
> Not every crack, shift, or uneven floor means structural damage in a Reno home. Some issues are cosmetic from normal settling and seasonal changes, while others signal foundation or framing movement. Use the signs below to tell the difference and know when an inspection is the safest next step. This guide covers the most common structural warning signs seen in Reno and Northern Nevada homes.

**Common Issues (5):**
1. Interior Wall and Ceiling Cracks — Cracks inside walls or ceilings may be cosmetic or structural depending on size, pattern, and change over time.
2. Doors or Windows Not Closing Properly — Sudden door or window misalignment can signal structural shifting caused by foundation or framing movement.
3. Uneven or Sloping Floors — Sloping or uneven floors may result from settling, weakened supports, or underlying structural issues.
4. Bowing or Separating Walls — Walls that bow or pull away from ceilings often point to structural stress requiring professional evaluation.
5. Water Intrusion Near the Foundation — Water near foundations can worsen soil movement and increase the risk of structural damage in Reno homes.

**Cosmetic (safe — 4 items):**
- Thin Hairline Cracks in Drywall — Small surface cracks caused by normal settling or temperature changes.
- Minor Slab Surface Cracking — Shallow concrete cracks that do not indicate foundation movement.
- Doors Sticking Seasonally — Door alignment changes linked to weather-related expansion and contraction.
- Small Nail Pops or Trim Gaps — Minor interior shifts often caused by aging materials or seasonal movement.

**Structural (inspect — 5 items):**
- Stair-Step Cracks in Brick or Block — Crack patterns that commonly signal foundation settlement or structural stress.
- Wide or Growing Foundation Cracks — Cracks that expand over time and may indicate active structural movement.
- Uneven or Sloping Floors — Floor changes that suggest compromised supports or shifting foundations.
- Bowing or Separating Walls — Wall movement that can result from sustained structural pressure.
- Doors and Windows Suddenly Misaligned — Rapid alignment changes often linked to underlying structural shifts.
